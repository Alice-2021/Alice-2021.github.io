The Indian Paintbrush

Scientific Name:

Castilleja

The Indian Paintbrush is the state flower of Wyoming. It is named after Spanish botanist Domingo Castillejo. The showy red structures are bracts, a type of modified leaf, and not petals. The actual flowers are greenish-yellowish-red. A variety of insects visit the flower, especially bees.Also called prairie fire, Indian paintbrush is a genus of about 200 species of annual and perennial herbaceous plants native to the west of the Americas from Alaska south to the Andes (as well as northeast Asia). It is found on rocky slopes and arid plains.
